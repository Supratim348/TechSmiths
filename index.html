A beginner-level Arduino-powered quadruped robot that mimics the walking gait of a spider. Built with servo motors, an ultrasonic sensor, and custom 3D-printed legs — this project explores the intersection of mechanics, electronics, and code.

Key Features

What makes QuadBot an exciting beginner robotics project

01

4-Legged Walking Gait

QuadBot uses a tripod or wave gait algorithm to move forward, backward, and turn. Each leg pair alternates to keep the robot balanced — just like a real spider or insect moves across terrain.

02

Obstacle Detection

An HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor mounted on the front detects objects up to 400 cm away. When an obstacle is within 15 cm, QuadBot stops and turns — basic autonomous navigation for beginners.

03

8 Servo Motor Control

Two SG90 servo motors per leg control the hip and knee joints. Controlled via the Arduino Servo library, each joint angle is mapped with precision to produce smooth, coordinated movement.

04

Portable Power Supply

Powered by a 7.4V LiPo battery regulated to 5V for servos and 3.3V for the Arduino. The compact power circuit ensures stable voltage even when all 8 servos are active simultaneously.

05

3D-Printed Chassis

The entire body frame and leg links were designed in Tinkercad and printed in PLA plastic. Lightweight yet strong, the chassis took roughly 6 hours to print at 20% infill density.

06

Bluetooth Remote Control

A HC-05 Bluetooth module lets you control QuadBot wirelessly from your phone using a simple serial app. Send characters (F/B/L/R/S) to command forward, back, turn, and stop actions.

Project Gallery

Step-by-step photos from planning to final assembly

VIEW
ELECTRONICS

Circuit Wiring & Breadboard

All 8 servo signal wires connected to Arduino PWM pins (3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 11) through a custom breadboard layout. The HC-SR04 trigger and echo pins are wired to digital pins 7 and 8.

VIEW
MECHANICAL

3D Printed Chassis & Legs

The body and leg links were designed in Tinkercad and sliced using Ultimaker Cura. Printed in white PLA at 0.2mm layer height, the parts required light sanding and assembly with M2 screws.

VIEW
FINAL BUILD

Assembled QuadBot

The completed robot with all components mounted, wiring managed with zip ties, and the Arduino loaded with the walking gait code. First successful walk achieved on Day 18 of the project.
